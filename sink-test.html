The Sink Test Explained

A simple, scientific way to verify the harvest maturity of your mangoes at home.

Science in Your Kitchen

A mango harvested at the right maturity has a higher physiological density than immature or force-ripened fruit.

As a mango matures on the tree, its starch content increases, making the fruit denser. When you place a correctly matured mango in water, it will sink or remain comfortably submerged. Immature mangoes that have been chemically "pushed" to look ripe will often float because they haven't developed this internal density.

How to Perform the Test

Try this with 1-2 firm mangoes from your box when they arrive.

1

Take a Firm Mango

Select a mango from the box while it is still firm (before natural ripening begins).

2

Place in Water

Gently place the mango into a deep bucket or sink filled with fresh water.

3

Observe the Density

Correctly matured mangoes will sink to the bottom or stay submerged. They won't "bob" on the surface.
